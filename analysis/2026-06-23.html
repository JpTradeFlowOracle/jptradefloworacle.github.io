M&A / Restructuring 15:30
Trial Holdings Inc. Ｇ－トライアル

Parent company streamlines resort business operations through a simple absorption-type split with a wholly-owned subsidiary.

Trial Holdings is absorbing the management operations of Trial Golf & Resort, a resort business currently held by its wholly-owned real estate subsidiary, Trial Real Estate. This internal reorganization aims to enhance group governance and decision-making efficiency by bringing the resort segment directly under the parent company's control.

This move reflects a strategic effort to optimize the group's diverse portfolio, particularly integrating the resort business more closely with the core operations, following recent expansion in the retail sector.

  • The absorption-type split is scheduled to become effective on August 1, 2026.
  • The transaction involves no consideration and will not alter the parent company's capital stock.
  • The transferred business includes 2,175 million JPY in fixed assets and 1,803 million JPY in total liabilities.
  • The resort management business being absorbed reported zero sales in the fiscal year ended June 2025.
Investor Takeaway Investors should view this as an internal governance enhancement and operational streamlining, with a minimal direct financial impact on consolidated results.

Tender Offer (TOB) 12:30
Nisshin Shoji Co., Ltd. 日新商

EDIAND's tender offer for Nisshin Shoji succeeds, leading to delisting.

The tender offer by EDIAND for Nisshin Shoji's common shares has successfully concluded, with tendered shares significantly surpassing the minimum threshold. This outcome establishes EDIAND as Nisshin Shoji's new parent company and largest shareholder, replacing ENEOS Holdings. The company intends to proceed with a squeeze-out, which will result in the delisting of Nisshin Shoji's shares from the Tokyo Stock Exchange Standard Market.

This transaction marks a fundamental shift in Nisshin Shoji's ownership, transitioning it to a private entity under EDIAND's control. The impending delisting will remove its shares from public trading, fundamentally altering its market presence and future strategic direction.

  • The tender offer by EDIAND for Nisshin Shoji concluded on June 22, 2026.
  • A total of 3,921,070 shares were tendered, exceeding the minimum required 2,291,500 shares.
  • EDIAND will become the new parent company and largest shareholder, holding 58.73% of voting rights effective June 29, 2026.
  • Nisshin Shoji is slated for delisting from the Tokyo Stock Exchange Standard Market following a planned squeeze-out process.
  • ENEOS Holdings, previously the largest shareholder with 17.08% of voting rights, will no longer hold that position.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the successful tender offer will lead to Nisshin Shoji's delisting, requiring them to tender their shares or be subject to a squeeze-out.

Management Change 13:00
Sanyodo Holdings Co., Ltd. 三洋堂ＨＤ

Sanyodo Holdings restructures for growth, establishing new business strategy department.

The company announced a significant organizational restructuring, replacing its business development unit with a new business strategy department. This change aims to accelerate sustainable growth and enhance corporate value through more rapid business expansion and strategic initiatives. A key executive has been appointed to lead this new strategic focus.

This strategic pivot signals a more proactive and integrated approach to future growth, moving beyond mere development to a comprehensive strategic framework for value creation.

  • Organizational changes and personnel appointments are effective June 23, 2026.
  • The former Business Development Department has been dissolved.
  • A new Business Strategy Department has been established.
  • Masayasu Kato transitions from leading the former Business Development Department to heading the new Business Strategy Department, with an elevated title.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note this shift towards a more strategic and accelerated approach to business expansion and value enhancement.
