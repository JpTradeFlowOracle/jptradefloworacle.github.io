Market Briefing

Today's corporate disclosures highlighted a mix of robust earnings performance and significant M&A activity. Several companies reported strong profit growth and upward forecast revisions, particularly in industrial and semiconductor-related sectors, often boosted by favorable currency trends. Concurrently, tender offers and internal restructurings signaled ongoing corporate portfolio optimization and competitive bidding dynamics, reflecting a dynamic corporate landscape.

The industrial and high-tech manufacturing sectors, particularly those serving semiconductor and global industrial demand, demonstrated strong profitability and upward forecast revisions. Meanwhile, the retail sector saw aggressive expansion strategies, while environmental services continued to show stable, record-setting performance. A competitive bidding situation emerged for Kakaku.com, with its board adopting a neutral stance on a tender offer due to a superior alternative proposal. Additionally, Osaka Organic Chemical and OSG significantly upgraded their profit outlooks, underscoring strong demand in their respective markets. The successful tender offer for Global Information will lead to its delisting, marking a significant ownership change.

M&A / Restructuring 15:30
Aica Kogyo Co., Ltd. アイカ工

Aica Kogyo to merge wholly-owned subsidiary to enhance operational efficiency.

Aica Kogyo announced an absorption-type merger with its wholly-owned, non-consolidated subsidiary, Sukou Service Co., Ltd. This strategic move aims to streamline manufacturing operations for synthetic resin products and improve overall management efficiency. As a simplified and short-form merger, it will proceed without requiring shareholder approval.

This internal restructuring reflects a focus on optimizing existing assets and consolidating production capabilities, potentially leading to cost synergies and improved operational agility within the group.

  • The merger involves Sukou Service Co., Ltd., a 100% owned, non-consolidated subsidiary.
  • The effective date for the merger is October 1, 2026.
  • Sukou Service's primary business is contract manufacturing of synthetic resin products.
  • No new shares or cash will be exchanged as part of the merger consideration.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note this internal consolidation is expected to have a minor impact on consolidated earnings but signals a commitment to operational optimization.

Tender Offer (TOB) 11:00
Kakaku.com, Inc. カカクコム

Board withdraws tender recommendation for initial TOB, adopts neutral stance due to higher competing offer.

Kakaku.com's board has revised its opinion on the ongoing tender offer by Kamgras 1, withdrawing its recommendation for shareholders to tender their shares and instead adopting a neutral position. This change follows the receipt of a new, legally binding acquisition proposal from a consortium led by Bain Capital and LINE Yahoo, offering a higher per-share price. The Special Committee also endorsed this shift to neutrality, acknowledging the superior terms of the alternative bid.

This development signals a competitive bidding situation for Kakaku.com, potentially leading to a higher acquisition price for shareholders. The board's neutrality empowers investors to evaluate the merits of both offers, reflecting enhanced corporate governance in response to a superior proposal.

  • Original board resolution on May 12, 2026, supported Kamgras 1's tender offer and recommended tendering.
  • A new, legally binding proposal was received on July 1, 2026, from a consortium led by Bain Capital and LINE Yahoo.
  • The new proposal offers JPY 3,384 per share, potentially rising to JPY 3,500 under certain conditions.
  • Kamgras 1's initial offer was JPY 3,000, later revised to JPY 3,250 and then JPY 3,300.
  • On July 1, 2026, the board maintained its support for the Kamgras 1 offer but withdrew its recommendation to tender, adopting a neutral stance.
Investor Takeaway Investors should carefully compare the terms of both tender offers, particularly the per-share price and conditions, as the board's neutral stance indicates a more attractive alternative is now on the table.

Tender Offer (TOB) 15:35
Global Information, Inc. グローバルＩ

Uzabase's tender offer for Global Information succeeds, leading to delisting.

Uzabase, Inc.'s tender offer for Global Information, Inc. has successfully concluded, with tendered shares significantly exceeding the minimum threshold. This outcome establishes Uzabase as the new parent company, holding over 93% of voting rights. The company intends to proceed with procedures to delist Global Information from the Standard market.

This successful tender offer fundamentally alters Global Information's ownership and corporate structure, transitioning it from a publicly traded entity to a private subsidiary of Uzabase, Inc. The impending delisting will remove its shares from public trading.

  • The tender offer by Uzabase, Inc. for Global Information, Inc. (4171) concluded on July 1, 2026.
  • The offer price was JPY 1,680 per common share.
  • A total of 2,775,740 shares were tendered, surpassing the minimum acquisition threshold of 1,983,600 shares.
  • Uzabase's post-tender offer ownership will be 93.29% of Global Information's voting rights.
  • The payment and ownership transfer are scheduled for July 8, 2026.
  • Global Information is expected to be delisted from the Standard market following the transaction.
Investor Takeaway Investors should be aware that Global Information will be delisted, and any remaining shareholders will likely be subject to a subsequent squeeze-out process.

Earnings Forecast 15:30
Osaka Organic Chemical Industry Ltd. 大有機化

Osaka Organic Chemical raises FY2026 earnings and dividend forecasts significantly.

The company revised its full-year and interim earnings forecasts upwards, driven by strong sales of semiconductor materials, particularly ArF photoresists, and high-purity special solvents. Improved profitability resulted from increased demand and successful price pass-through for rising costs, leading to substantial increases across all profit metrics.

This revision highlights robust demand in key electronics and semiconductor sectors, indicating strong operational performance and effective cost management in a challenging environment.

  • FY2026 Q2 consolidated net profit forecast increased by 38.6% to JPY 3,050 million.
  • FY2026 full-year consolidated operating profit forecast increased by 17.2% to JPY 7,500 million.
  • Annual dividend forecast for FY2026 raised by JPY 6.00 to JPY 86.00 per share.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the company's strong performance in critical growth markets and its commitment to increased shareholder returns through higher dividends.

Earnings Forecast 15:30
OSG Corporation ＯＳＧ

OSG significantly raises Q2 profit forecast on strong demand, favorable FX.

OSG Corporation has substantially increased its second-quarter earnings forecast for the fiscal year ending November 2026, driven by a weaker yen and robust demand across key industrial sectors globally. The upward revision reflects strong performance in North America, Europe, and Asia, offsetting some weakness in the automotive segment.

This revision highlights OSG's successful diversification strategy and operational efficiency in capitalizing on global industrial growth, particularly in high-value sectors like aerospace, medical, and semiconductor-related industries.

  • Revised Q2 FY2026 net profit forecast is JPY 12.5 billion, up 73.6% from the previous JPY 7.2 billion.
  • Operating profit forecast increased by 50.7% to JPY 15.6 billion.
  • Sales forecast raised by 13.2% to JPY 92.0 billion for the cumulative second quarter.
  • The revision covers the period from December 1, 2025, to May 31, 2026.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the strong underlying demand trends in OSG's key markets and the positive impact of currency movements and efficient raw material procurement on profitability.

Earnings Report 14:20
Nichiryoku Co., Ltd. ニチリョク

Nichiryoku corrects FY2026 earnings, significantly reducing net loss by 35.7%.

Nichiryoku has issued a correction to its non-consolidated earnings report for the fiscal year ended March 2026, primarily due to a re-evaluation of security deposit estimates. The revised figures show a substantial improvement in profitability, with the net loss for the period reduced by 35.7%, alongside increases in total assets and net assets. The company's financial outlook for the next fiscal year remains unchanged in absolute terms.

This accounting adjustment presents a more favorable financial picture for the past fiscal year, potentially enhancing investor confidence in the company's balance sheet and profitability metrics. While the underlying business operations for the period are not re-evaluated, the improved reported figures could influence market perception.

  • Net loss for the fiscal year ended March 2026 was corrected from JPY 207 million to JPY 133 million, a 35.7% reduction.
  • Operating loss for the same period improved by 13.3%, from JPY 485 million to JPY 420 million.
  • Net assets increased by JPY 72 million (2.5%) to JPY 2,915 million.
  • Revenue for the fiscal year was slightly revised upwards by JPY 15 million (0.9%) to JPY 1,725 million.
  • The company's absolute financial forecast for the fiscal year ending March 2027 remains unchanged.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the improved profitability and balance sheet figures for FY2026, stemming from accounting adjustments, while recognizing that the company's forward-looking business projections are stable.

Earnings Report 15:30
e-supportlink Co., Ltd. イーサポート

e-supportlink reports strong Q2 profit growth, maintains full-year outlook with anticipated net income moderation.

e-supportlink achieved substantial profit growth in its second quarter, with operating profit more than doubling and net income attributable to shareholders increasing significantly. This performance was driven by solid revenue expansion across both operational and agricultural support segments. Despite the strong first-half results, the company has maintained its previously announced full-year forecast, which anticipates a slight decline in full-year net income, suggesting a conservative outlook for the latter half of the fiscal year.

The robust profit expansion in the first half highlights the company's effective strategy in enhancing operational efficiencies and expanding its market presence in fresh produce distribution. However, the unchanged full-year net income forecast, implying a weaker second half, may signal potential future challenges or a cautious management stance.

  • Net sales for the second quarter reached 3,351 million JPY, an increase of 10.2% year-over-year.
  • Operating profit surged by 111.1% to 124 million JPY.
  • Net income attributable to parent company shareholders increased by 145.9% to 48 million JPY.
  • The full-year net income forecast remains unchanged at 144 million JPY, representing a 1.7% decrease from the previous fiscal year.
  • A new subsidiary, Marche Plus Co., Ltd., was established and consolidated during the period, contributing to the operational support segment.
Investor Takeaway Investors should weigh the impressive first-half profit growth against the unchanged, slightly lower full-year net income forecast, which suggests a cautious outlook for the remainder of the fiscal year despite current momentum.

Earnings Report 15:30
Kusuri no Aoki Holdings Co., Ltd. クスリのアオキＨＤ

Kusuri no Aoki posts robust revenue growth, lower net profit amid aggressive expansion.

The company reported substantial revenue growth, driven by aggressive store expansion and strategic acquisitions in the food retail sector. However, this top-line expansion was accompanied by a modest decline in net profit attributable to shareholders, with operating and ordinary profits showing only marginal increases. This indicates that the rapid growth strategy, while boosting sales, is currently impacting profitability due to associated costs.

The company's aggressive expansion, including M&A in the food retail sector, signals a strong push for market share and diversification beyond traditional drugstores, aiming for a "food & drug" one-stop shopping model. This strategy is intensifying competition in the retail sector and could reshape regional market dynamics.

  • Full-year revenue for the period ending May 2026 increased by 13.0% to JPY 566.8 billion.
  • Net profit attributable to shareholders declined by 3.7% to JPY 17.1 billion.
  • The company opened 111 new drugstores and 38 new dispensing pharmacies, and acquired 37 supermarket stores.
  • The annual dividend for the period increased to JPY 56.00 per share, including a JPY 40.00 special dividend.
  • Significant share repurchases and cancellations were executed, reducing outstanding shares.
  • For the next fiscal year, the company forecasts a 12.9% increase in revenue and a 10.9% increase in net profit.
Investor Takeaway Investors should monitor whether the company's aggressive expansion and M&A strategy can translate into sustainable profit growth and improved margins in the coming periods, especially given the current pressure on net income despite strong sales.

Earnings Report 15:30
Daiseki Co., Ltd. ダイセキ

Daiseki reports record Q1 profits, driven by core waste treatment and environmental solutions.

Daiseki achieved record-high first-quarter revenue and profits, driven by strong performance in its core industrial waste treatment and environmental solutions businesses. Strategic efforts to secure recycling materials and focus on high-value projects successfully offset rising costs and some project delays in other segments, leading to robust earnings growth.

The company's strong start to the fiscal year, marked by record profitability and a high equity ratio, underscores its stable operational execution and resilience in the environmental services sector, signaling potential for sustained growth.

  • First-quarter revenue increased 2.0% year-over-year to ¥18,275 million.
  • Net profit attributable to parent company shareholders rose 17.6% to ¥2,871 million, with EPS of ¥60.73.
  • The company maintained its full-year earnings and dividend forecasts, projecting a ¥86.00 annual dividend.
  • Total assets reached ¥106,080 million, with a high equity ratio of 78.1%.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note Daiseki's consistent profitability and strong balance sheet, which support its unchanged full-year growth and dividend projections.
