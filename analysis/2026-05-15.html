Kyokuyo Co., Ltd. 極洋

Kyokuyo abolishes its anti-takeover defense, aligning with evolving governance standards.

Kyokuyo's board has resolved to discontinue its existing anti-takeover defense mechanism, effective at the conclusion of its upcoming annual general meeting. This decision reflects a comprehensive review considering changes in the social and economic environment, developments in M&A regulations, and enhanced dialogue with institutional investors, leading the company to conclude the current plan is no longer necessary.

This move signals Kyokuyo's commitment to modern corporate governance principles and a more open stance towards potential M&A activity. It aligns with broader market expectations for Japanese companies to dismantle such defenses, potentially enhancing investor confidence and market liquidity.

  • Board resolved to abolish the current anti-takeover plan on May 15, 2026.
  • Abolition will be effective at the conclusion of the 103rd Ordinary General Meeting of Shareholders, scheduled for June 24, 2026.
  • The current anti-takeover plan was initially approved by shareholders on June 27, 2023.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note this as a positive governance development, indicating a more shareholder-friendly approach and potentially increasing the company's attractiveness to a wider range of investors.

M&A / Restructuring 13:00
DIJET Industrial Co., Ltd. ダイジェット

DIJET renews anti-takeover defense, enhancing shareholder protection and governance.

DIJET Industrial has decided to continue its anti-takeover defense policy, subject to shareholder approval at the upcoming 100th Annual General Meeting in June 2026. The updated plan includes minor revisions to the scope of target share purchases and criteria for identifying concerted actions, aiming to ensure fair value and sufficient time for shareholders to evaluate any large-scale acquisition proposals. The company emphasizes its alignment with recent corporate governance codes and guidelines.

This renewal signals DIJET's proactive stance on corporate governance and shareholder protection, potentially deterring opportunistic bids while ensuring a structured process for evaluating legitimate offers. The explicit rejection of dead-hand/slow-hand provisions reinforces its commitment to shareholder will.

  • The anti-takeover policy, originally introduced in 2008, will be renewed for another three years, pending shareholder approval in June 2026.
  • The plan's threshold for a 'large-scale purchase' remains at 20% or more of voting rights.
  • Key revisions include adjustments to the scope of target share purchases and criteria for identifying joint/concerted actions.
  • The Board of Directors unanimously approved the continuation, including all Audit & Supervisory Committee members.
  • No large-scale purchase proposals have been received by the company as of the filing date.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note DIJET's continued commitment to robust governance and its readiness to protect shareholder interests against potentially coercive takeovers, while maintaining flexibility for legitimate offers.
