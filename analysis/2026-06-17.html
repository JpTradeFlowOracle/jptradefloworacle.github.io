PostPrime Inc. Ｇ－ポストプライム

PostPrime acquires M&A brokerage firm Infinity Life, entering new growth domain.

PostPrime, operator of the financial and economic information platform "PostPrime," is acquiring Infinity Life, an M&A and real estate brokerage firm, making it a wholly-owned subsidiary. This strategic move marks PostPrime's entry into the M&A brokerage sector, leveraging its existing user base to offer practical, value-added services beyond information provision. The acquisition aims to establish a dual revenue stream and capitalize on the growing demand for business succession M&A in Japan.

This acquisition diversifies PostPrime's business model beyond content and advertising, tapping into the structurally growing Japanese business succession M&A market. It enhances user monetization by converting platform engagement into transactional services, potentially improving PostPrime's overall revenue stability and growth trajectory.

  • PostPrime decided to acquire 100% of Infinity Life's shares on June 17, 2026, with a planned execution date of July 1, 2026.
  • The total acquisition cost is JPY 105 million, comprising JPY 100 million for shares and JPY 5 million for advisory fees.
  • Infinity Life reported JPY 129 million in revenue and JPY 24 million in ordinary profit for the fiscal year ended March 2025.
  • PostPrime's SNS platform currently boasts approximately 430,000 users, which will serve as a channel for M&A deal sourcing.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note PostPrime's strategic pivot to diversify revenue streams and capitalize on the growing M&A brokerage market, potentially improving monetization of its existing user base and enhancing long-term growth prospects.

eWeLL Inc. Ｇ－ｅＷｅＬＬ

CEO-related entity increases stake in eWeLL, crossing 5% ownership threshold.

An entity associated with eWeLL's CEO has acquired additional shares from the CEO, bringing its total ownership to 5.64%. This transaction is classified under regulations governing significant share accumulations, signaling a consolidation of control by the CEO's related party.

This move enhances the stability of the company's shareholding structure by strengthening the CEO's influence through a dedicated entity, potentially impacting future strategic decisions and governance.

  • iBow Inc., an entity related to eWeLL's CEO, acquired 60,000 shares.
  • The acquisition increases iBow Inc.'s total holding to 860,000 shares.
  • Post-acquisition, iBow Inc. holds 5.64% of eWeLL's total voting rights.
  • The transaction occurred on June 17, 2026.
  • iBow Inc. states its intention to be a long-term, stable shareholder.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the increased concentration of ownership by a CEO-affiliated entity, reinforcing management's long-term commitment and control.
