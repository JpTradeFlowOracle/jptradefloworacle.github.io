Market Briefing

Today's corporate landscape in Japan was characterized by active M&A, with several tender offers concluding successfully and one prominent online platform becoming the subject of a competitive bidding situation. Concurrently, a number of companies reported robust interim financial results, leading to significant upward revisions in their full-year profit and dividend forecasts. These developments underscore ongoing strategic restructuring and strong operational performance in specific segments of the Japanese market.

The electrical construction sector is undergoing consolidation, highlighted by a successful tender offer aiming for full ownership and delisting. In the online services and information sector, a competitive bidding scenario has emerged for a key player, while other firms demonstrated strong profitability driven by efficiency and AI integration. The energy and engineering sectors also contributed to positive earnings revisions, signaling robust project execution. Kinden Corporation successfully completed its tender offer for Kodensha, signaling a strategic move towards full acquisition and delisting in the electrical construction industry. Separately, Kamgras 1 extended its tender offer for Kakaku.com, but the target's board shifted to a neutral stance due to a higher, competing acquisition proposal, indicating a potential bidding war. Additionally, note Inc., KG Information, and SALA Corporation all announced substantial upward revisions to their full-year profit forecasts, driven by strong operational performance and efficiency gains.

Tender Offer (TOB) 15:30
Kinden Corporation きんでん

Kinden successfully completes tender offer for Kodensha, aiming for full acquisition and delisting.

Kinden Corporation has successfully concluded its tender offer for Kodensha Co., Ltd., acquiring 3,714,499 shares. This outcome makes Kodensha an affiliate of Kinden, with the combined ownership of Kinden and its related parties exceeding 90%. Kinden plans to acquire all remaining shares and delist Kodensha from the Tokyo Stock Exchange Standard Market.

This move consolidates Kinden's position in the electrical construction sector by integrating Kodensha, signaling a strategic shift towards full ownership and operational control. The delisting will remove Kodensha from public trading, impacting its market visibility.

  • Kinden acquired 3,714,499 shares of Kodensha.
  • The offer price was 11,501 JPY per share.
  • Kinden's post-tender offer ownership is 42.53% of Kodensha's voting rights.
  • Kodensha will become an affiliate of Kinden on July 13, 2026.
  • Kinden intends to acquire all remaining shares and delist Kodensha.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note Kodensha's impending delisting, which will restrict future public trading opportunities, and Kinden's strengthened market position through this acquisition.

Tender Offer (TOB) 15:30
KODENSHA Co., Ltd. 弘電社

Kinden's tender offer for Kodensha succeeds, leading to delisting.

Kinden Corporation successfully concluded its tender offer for Kodensha, acquiring 3,714,499 shares, which exceeded the minimum acceptance threshold. This transaction will result in Kodensha becoming a consolidated subsidiary of Kinden, with delisting from the Standard Market anticipated.

This acquisition strengthens Kinden's market position by integrating Kodensha's electrical and telecommunications infrastructure operations. The planned delisting signifies a full transition to private ownership under Kinden's control, allowing for deeper strategic alignment.

  • The tender offer period ran from May 26, 2026, to July 6, 2026.
  • 3,714,499 shares were tendered, surpassing the minimum acceptance condition of 1,336,800 shares.
  • The offer price was JPY 11,501 per share.
  • Kinden's ownership in Kodensha will increase to 42.53% of voting rights post-settlement on July 13, 2026.
  • Kodensha is expected to be delisted from the Standard Market following the transaction.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note that Kodensha will transition to a consolidated subsidiary of Kinden and will be delisted, ending its public trading.

Tender Offer (TOB) 16:24
Kamgras 1 Corporation Ｋａｍｇｒａｓ１株式会社

Kamgras 1 extends tender offer, Kakaku.com board neutralizes recommendation due to higher bid.

Kamgras 1 has extended its tender offer for Kakaku.com until July 22, 2026. This adjustment follows Kakaku.com's board revising its stance from recommending the offer to adopting a neutral position, prompted by a new, higher legally binding acquisition proposal from another party.

This development signals a competitive bidding environment for Kakaku.com, potentially leading to a higher acquisition price and increased shareholder value.

  • Tender offer period for Kamgras 1's bid extended from July 16, 2026, to July 22, 2026, totaling 50 business days.
  • Kakaku.com's board of directors withdrew its recommendation for shareholders to tender, adopting a neutral stance.
  • A competing, legally binding tender offer proposal of JPY 3,384 per share (potentially JPY 3,500) has been received by Kakaku.com.
  • The current tender offer price by Kamgras 1 remains JPY 3,000 per common share.
  • The settlement start date for Kamgras 1's offer is delayed from July 24, 2026, to July 29, 2026.
Investor Takeaway Investors in Kakaku.com should assess the current JPY 3,000 offer against the new, higher competing bid and consider the possibility of further price revisions.

This adjustment follows Kakaku.com's recent amendment to its opinion statement and Kamgras 1's subsequent filing of an amended tender offer registration, providing shareholders additional time to consider the offer.

The extension suggests the acquirer is ensuring all regulatory requirements are met and allowing ample time for shareholder response, potentially to secure sufficient participation following recent disclosures.

  • The tender offer period for Kakaku.com shares is extended from July 16, 2026, to July 22, 2026.
  • The total tender offer period will now span 50 business days, an increase from the previously announced 47 business days.
  • The settlement start date for the tender offer has been revised from July 24, 2026, to July 29, 2026.
  • The extension was initiated after Kakaku.com amended its opinion statement on July 2, 2026, prompting Kamgras 1 to file an amended tender offer registration on July 7, 2026.

Earnings Forecast 15:30
KG Information ＫＧ情報

KG Information significantly beats interim profit forecasts, revising full-year outlook upwards.

The company reported interim results significantly exceeding its previous forecasts, driven by robust performance in its lifestyle information segment and a gain from investment security sales. This strong interim performance has led to an upward revision of the full-year earnings forecast across all profit metrics.

The substantial beat, particularly in net profit, signals strong operational execution and effective asset management. The upward revision of the full-year outlook suggests sustained positive momentum, which could positively influence investor sentiment.

  • Interim net profit attributable to the parent company reached JPY 278 million, significantly exceeding the JPY 155 million forecast.
  • Interim operating profit of JPY 246 million surpassed the JPY 228 million forecast.
  • The full-year net profit forecast was revised upwards by 34.7% to JPY 479 million from the previous JPY 356 million.
  • The company attributed the interim outperformance to strong visitor numbers and contract completions in its 'House Building School' business, alongside a gain from investment security sales.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the significant profit outperformance driven by both operational strength and non-operating gains, leading to a materially improved full-year outlook.
