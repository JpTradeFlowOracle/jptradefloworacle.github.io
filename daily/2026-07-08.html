What is EDINET and why is this data important?

EDINET (Electronic Disclosure for Investors NETwork) is the official disclosure platform operated by Japan's Financial Services Agency. It contains mandatory filings including tender offers, large shareholder reports (5%+ ownership changes), extraordinary reports, and share buyback disclosures. JP Disclosure Intelligence structures and translates this data into English daily.

M&A / Restructuring Tempos Holdings Co., Ltd.

Tempos Holdings expands beauty segment with Tomi Beauty Salon acquisition via stock exchange.

Tempos Holdings, primarily known for restaurant support, is acquiring Tomi Beauty Salon through a stock exchange to bolster its nascent beauty business, which began in 2025. Tender Offer (TOB) Kakaku.com, Inc.

Kakaku.com board withdraws tender offer recommendation amid competing bid.

Kakaku.com's board has shifted its stance on the ongoing tender offer by Kamgras 1 Corporation, withdrawing its recommendation for shareholders to tender their shares and adopting a neutral position. This change follows the emergence of a higher, competing acquisition proposal from another bidder, offering a significantly increased price per share. The tender offer period for Kamgras 1 has also been extended, providing more time for shareholders to evaluate the evolving situation.

This development signals a potential bidding war for Kakaku.com, creating uncertainty for the existing tender offer and highlighting the board's commitment to maximizing shareholder value by considering alternative proposals.

  • The board maintains its approval of the tender offer but no longer recommends tendering shares.
  • The tender offer period for Kamgras 1 has been extended from July 16, 2026, to July 22, 2026.
  • A competing proposal offers JPY 3,384 per share, potentially rising to JPY 3,500, compared to Kamgras 1's JPY 3,000 offer.
  • Oasis Management Company Ltd. has agreed to tender its 38,200,548 shares to the competing bidder.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should carefully consider the board's neutral stance and the higher competing offer, as tendering at the current JPY 3,000 price may not be optimal given the potential for a revised bid or a successful alternative acquisition.

Tender Offer (TOB) Medipal Holdings Corporation

Medipal Holdings successfully completes PALTAC tender offer, achieving over 92% ownership.

Medipal Holdings has successfully concluded its tender offer for PALTAC, acquiring 24,466,104 shares at JPY 6,650 each. This transaction significantly boosts Medipal's ownership in PALTAC to 92.64%, surpassing the initial minimum acquisition threshold. The successful offer paves the way for PALTAC's full privatization and eventual delisting.

This move solidifies Medipal's control over PALTAC, enabling streamlined operations and strategic integration without minority shareholder concerns. The impending delisting will remove PALTAC from public trading, reflecting a strategic shift towards a fully consolidated subsidiary.

  • The tender offer concluded on July 7, 2026.
  • Medipal Holdings acquired 24,466,104 shares of PALTAC.
  • The acquisition price per share was JPY 6,650.
  • Medipal Holdings' ownership in PALTAC increased from 52.40% to 92.64%.
  • A squeeze-out process is planned, leading to PALTAC's delisting.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note that PALTAC shares will be delisted following the planned squeeze-out, transitioning it into a wholly-owned subsidiary of Medipal Holdings.

Tender Offer (TOB) G-Storage Oh Co., Ltd.

Area Link launches tender offer for G-Storage Oh, aiming for full ownership and delisting.

Area Link has initiated a tender offer to acquire all outstanding shares and stock options of G-Storage Oh, seeking to make it a wholly-owned subsidiary and delist it. The target company's board supports the offer and recommends shareholders tender their shares, citing strategic synergies with Area Link and the challenges of meeting new listing maintenance standards. The offer price of 1,340 yen per share represents a significant premium over recent market prices, following multiple rounds of negotiation with an independent special committee.

This transaction highlights the ongoing trend of listed Japanese companies going private, often driven by the desire to streamline operations, reduce listing costs, and address new, stricter listing maintenance requirements. For G-Storage Oh, it offers a strategic path to enhance its storage business through integration with a larger industry player.

  • The tender offer price for common shares is 1,340 yen.
  • The offer period is from July 9, 2026, to August 21, 2026 (30 business days).
  • The minimum tender condition is 1,291,700 shares, representing 66.67% of voting rights, to facilitate a squeeze-out.
  • The offer price provides a premium ranging from 36.87% to 42.86% over G-Storage Oh's recent stock prices.
  • An independent special committee was established, engaging independent financial and legal advisors, and negotiated the price up from an initial 1,255 yen.
  • G-Storage Oh faces challenges meeting the 10 billion yen market capitalization standard for Growth market companies by March 1, 2030.

Investor TakeawayInvestors in G-Storage Oh should consider tendering their shares at the offered price of 1,340 yen, as the board recommends it, the price includes a substantial premium, and delisting is highly probable.

Tender Offer (TOB) Arealink Co., Ltd.

Arealink launches tender offer for Storage Oh to accelerate storage business expansion.

Arealink is initiating a tender offer to acquire Storage Oh, a self-storage operator with approximately 13,000 rooms, aiming to make it a wholly-owned subsidiary. This acquisition is a strategic move to achieve Arealink's mid-term goal of operating 200,000 storage rooms by 2029, leveraging synergies in operational efficiency, customer acquisition, and data analytics. The move is expected to enhance group corporate value and consolidate market share.

This acquisition significantly strengthens Arealink's position in the competitive Japanese self-storage market, expanding its operational footprint and accelerating its growth trajectory through M&A, a key component of its strategic plan. It demonstrates a clear intent to consolidate and achieve scale.

  • Arealink targets 200,000 total storage rooms by 2029.
  • The tender offer price for common shares is 1,340 JPY per share, representing a 42.25% premium over the previous day's closing price.
  • Storage Oh operates approximately 13,000 rooms, primarily in the Tokyo metropolitan area.
  • The total acquisition cost is approximately 2.6 billion JPY, funded by Arealink's own capital.
  • The offer period runs from July 9, 2026, to August 21, 2026.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note Arealink's aggressive M&A strategy to drive growth and market share in the self-storage sector, with a clear path to achieving its ambitious room count targets and enhancing profitability.
