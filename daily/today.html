M&A / Restructuring Mitsubishi Corporation

Mitsubishi Corporation streamlines Canadian salmon farming operations through subsidiary merger.

Mitsubishi Corporation's wholly-owned subsidiary, Cermaq Group AS, is merging its Canadian salmon farming and sales subsidiaries. M&A / Restructuring Okamoto Glass Co., Ltd.

Okamoto Glass renews its anti-takeover defense plan, emphasizing shareholder value and fair process.

Okamoto Glass has decided to continue its existing anti-takeover defense plan, originally introduced in 2007, until the conclusion of its 2027 Annual General Meeting. The plan aims to protect corporate value and shareholder interests by ensuring sufficient time and information for the board and shareholders to evaluate any large-scale share purchase proposals, thereby preventing coercive or detrimental acquisitions. Its continuation is contingent upon shareholder approval at the upcoming general meeting.

This renewal signals the company's proactive stance on corporate governance and shareholder protection, potentially deterring opportunistic takeovers while committing to a transparent evaluation process for legitimate offers.

  • The anti-takeover plan, first introduced in June 2007, is now renewed until the conclusion of the 2027 Annual General Meeting.
  • Countermeasures, primarily a free allotment of stock acquisition rights, are triggered if an acquirer's shareholding reaches 20% without prior board approval.
  • An independent Special Committee, composed of external experts, oversees the board's decisions regarding the plan's activation and operation.
  • The plan requires shareholder approval for its continuation and can be unilaterally abolished by the board at any time.
  • As of March 31, 2026, the top 10 shareholders collectively hold 26.95% of the outstanding shares.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note the company's commitment to a structured defense against hostile takeovers, which prioritizes long-term value and shareholder input through a transparent process and independent oversight.

M&A / Restructuring Sansei Co., Ltd.

Sansei extends takeover defense, emphasizing shareholder value and independent oversight.

Sansei Co., Ltd. announced its intention to continue its existing takeover defense policy, subject to shareholder approval at the upcoming general meeting. The revised plan, which includes updated trigger definitions, aims to protect corporate value and shareholder interests by ensuring adequate time and information for evaluating large-scale acquisition proposals, while also deterring hostile bids deemed detrimental.

This move by a Standard market company highlights a proactive approach to corporate governance and shareholder protection in Japan, aligning with recent national guidelines. It signals the company's commitment to maintaining strategic independence and ensuring fair value for shareholders in potential acquisition scenarios.

  • The current takeover defense plan, approved June 29, 2023, was set to expire at the 71st Ordinary General Meeting of Shareholders (scheduled around June 25, 2026).
  • The Board of Directors resolved to continue the plan, extending its validity until the conclusion of the Ordinary General Meeting scheduled for June 2029, pending shareholder approval.
  • The plan's trigger for defensive measures is a large-scale purchase of 20% or more of the company's shares.
  • The defense mechanism involves a free allotment of stock acquisition rights to existing shareholders, excluding the acquirer.
  • An Independent Committee, comprising outside directors, outside auditors, and external experts, plays a crucial role in evaluating proposals and recommending actions to the Board.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note the continuation of this robust, guideline-compliant takeover defense, which prioritizes shareholder decision-making and independent oversight, while potentially diluting an unwelcome acquirer's stake.

  • E Smart Energy, established in January 2026, is set to acquire an LP gas sales business on June 29, 2026.

Tender Offer (TOB) G-Manabieid Co., Ltd.

NOVA Holdings successfully completes tender offer, becoming G-Manabieid's new parent company.

NOVA Holdings has successfully concluded its tender offer for G-Manabieid shares, acquiring a controlling stake. The acquisition, combined with an off-market purchase from a previous major shareholder, will make NOVA Holdings the new direct parent company with over 50% of voting rights. This transaction solidifies the strategic alignment between the two education technology firms.

This move integrates G-Manabieid more deeply into NOVA Holdings' educational ecosystem, potentially enhancing synergy in their respective tutoring and content businesses. The company's listing status is expected to be maintained, indicating a strategic acquisition rather than a full privatization.

  • The tender offer concluded on May 28, 2026, with a purchase price of JPY 338 per share.
  • NOVA Holdings will acquire 624,100 shares through the tender offer and an additional 1,138,300 shares off-market.
  • Upon settlement on June 4, 2026, NOVA Holdings will hold 51.63% of G-Manabieid's voting rights.
  • Inayoshi Capital Partners, previously a major shareholder, will indirectly retain its stake through NOVA Holdings.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note the change in direct control to NOVA Holdings, while G-Manabieid's shares are expected to remain publicly traded on the Growth market.
