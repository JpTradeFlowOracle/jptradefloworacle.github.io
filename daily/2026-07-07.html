What is EDINET and why is this data important?

EDINET (Electronic Disclosure for Investors NETwork) is the official disclosure platform operated by Japan's Financial Services Agency. It contains mandatory filings including tender offers, large shareholder reports (5%+ ownership changes), extraordinary reports, and share buyback disclosures. JP Disclosure Intelligence structures and translates this data into English daily.

Tender Offer (TOB) Kinden Corporation

Kinden successfully completes tender offer for Kodensha, aiming for full acquisition and delisting.

Kinden Corporation has successfully concluded its tender offer for Kodensha Co., Ltd., acquiring 3,714,499 shares. This outcome makes Kodensha an affiliate of Kinden, with the combined ownership of Kinden and its related parties exceeding 90%. Kinden plans to acquire all remaining shares and delist Kodensha from the Tokyo Stock Exchange Standard Market.

This move consolidates Kinden's position in the electrical construction sector by integrating Kodensha, signaling a strategic shift towards full ownership and operational control. The delisting will remove Kodensha from public trading, impacting its market visibility.

  • Kinden acquired 3,714,499 shares of Kodensha.
  • The offer price was 11,501 JPY per share.
  • Kinden's post-tender offer ownership is 42.53% of Kodensha's voting rights.
  • Kodensha will become an affiliate of Kinden on July 13, 2026.
  • Kinden intends to acquire all remaining shares and delist Kodensha.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note Kodensha's impending delisting, which will restrict future public trading opportunities, and Kinden's strengthened market position through this acquisition.

Tender Offer (TOB) KODENSHA Co., Ltd.

Kinden's tender offer for Kodensha succeeds, leading to delisting.

Kinden Corporation successfully concluded its tender offer for Kodensha, acquiring 3,714,499 shares, which exceeded the minimum acceptance threshold. This transaction will result in Kodensha becoming a consolidated subsidiary of Kinden, with delisting from the Standard Market anticipated.

This acquisition strengthens Kinden's market position by integrating Kodensha's electrical and telecommunications infrastructure operations. The planned delisting signifies a full transition to private ownership under Kinden's control, allowing for deeper strategic alignment.

  • 3,714,499 shares were tendered, surpassing the minimum acceptance condition of 1,336,800 shares.
  • The offer price was JPY 11,501 per share.
  • Kinden's ownership in Kodensha will increase to 42.53% of voting rights post-settlement on July 13, 2026.
  • Kodensha is expected to be delisted from the Standard Market following the transaction.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note that Kodensha will transition to a consolidated subsidiary of Kinden and will be delisted, ending its public trading.

Tender Offer (TOB) Kamgras 1 Corporation

Kamgras 1 extends tender offer, Kakaku.com board neutralizes recommendation due to higher bid.

Kamgras 1 has extended its tender offer for Kakaku.com until July 22, 2026. This adjustment follows Kakaku.com's board revising its stance from recommending the offer to adopting a neutral position, prompted by a new, higher legally binding acquisition proposal from another party.

This development signals a competitive bidding environment for Kakaku.com, potentially leading to a higher acquisition price and increased shareholder value.

  • Tender offer period for Kamgras 1's bid extended from July 16, 2026, to July 22, 2026, totaling 50 business days.
  • Kakaku.com's board of directors withdrew its recommendation for shareholders to tender, adopting a neutral stance.
  • A competing, legally binding tender offer proposal of JPY 3,384 per share (potentially JPY 3,500) has been received by Kakaku.com.
  • The current tender offer price by Kamgras 1 remains JPY 3,000 per common share.
  • The settlement start date for Kamgras 1's offer is delayed from July 24, 2026, to July 29, 2026.

Investor TakeawayInvestors in Kakaku.com should assess the current JPY 3,000 offer against the new, higher competing bid and consider the possibility of further price revisions.

  • The settlement start date for the tender offer has been revised from July 24, 2026, to July 29, 2026.
  • The extension was initiated after Kakaku.com amended its opinion statement on July 2, 2026, prompting Kamgras 1 to file an amended tender offer registration on July 7, 2026.

Investor TakeawayInvestors should note the revised tender offer timeline and assess the implications of the extended period for their participation decisions.
